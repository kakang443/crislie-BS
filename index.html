History of Siquijor Folk Healers and Herbs

Siquijor Island, located in the Visayas region of the Philippines, is renowned for its rich tradition of folk healing, deeply rooted in both indigenous and Catholic practices. Central to this tradition are the mananambal, traditional healers who utilize a combination of herbal medicine, spiritual rituals, and prayer to address both physical and spiritual ailments.

The term mananambal is derived from the Cebuano word panambal, meaning "to heal." These practitioners often inherit their knowledge through family lineage, apprenticeship, or spiritual calling. Their methods encompass a blend of herbal remedies, prayer (orasyon), and rituals to treat various conditions, including those believed to be caused by supernatural forces. A significant aspect of their practice involves the pangalap—a ritualistic gathering of medicinal plants. This occurs over seven Fridays during Lent, culminating on Good Friday. Healers venture into sacred sites, such as forests and caves, to collect herbs, barks, and other natural elements, following strict spiritual protocols. These ingredients are then used to create potent healing concoctions.

Siquijor's healers utilize a diverse array of plant species, many of which are indigenous to the island. Research has identified up to 218 plant species employed in traditional healing practices, predominantly from families like Apocynaceae, Euphorbiaceae, Lamiaceae, Rubiaceae, Fabaceae, and Zingiberaceae. Commonly used plants include Mangagaw (Euphorbia hirta) for treating conditions like dengue fever, Dapdap (Erythrina variegata) for ailments such as hemorrhoids, Tuba-Tuba (Jatropha curcas) applied in the treatment of arthritis, Wachichao (Orthosiphon aristatus) known for its benefits to kidney health, Sabana (Soursop) investigated for its potential anti-cancer properties, and Kipi-kipi (Biophytum sensitivum) recognized for its fatigue-relieving effects.

Siquijor's Folk Healing Festival, held annually during Holy Week, is a vibrant celebration of the island's healing traditions. During this period, mananambal gather to prepare medicinal potions using over 200 plant species. The festival features rituals such as tuob (herbal steam therapy) and bulo-bulo (a spiritual cleansing involving water and prayer), blending indigenous practices with Catholic elements.

Juanita Torremocha

Juanita Torremocha, a traditional healer from Siquijor, uses a combination of herbal and spiritual practices to treat her patients. Her methods include tuob, hilot, lana, and tawal—each a unique practice to restore energy and promote healing.

Felinor Q. Magpiyong

Felinor Q. Magpiyong is a respected folk healer in Siquijor known for his expertise in tawas healing — a traditional Filipino spiritual cleansing ritual that uses alum crystals to diagnose and remove negative energies or illnesses through prayer and ritualistic brushing. His healing sessions are deeply rooted in faith and local customs, helping many find relief and balance.

Vicenti Macalisang

Vicenti Macalisang is a well-known folk healer in Siquijor who practices spiritual healing, healing massage, and hand reading. Combining faith-based rituals with physical therapy and intuitive insights through hand analysis, he offers holistic treatments aimed at restoring both body and spirit. His approach reflects the rich tradition of Siquijor’s healing heritage.

Rufino Macalib-og

Rufino Macalib-og is a well-known local healer who helps people through traditional methods passed down through generations. He uses tawas to find out what’s causing someone’s illness—often things that can’t be seen, like bad energy or spirits. With gentle healing massages, protective tawal rituals, and deep spiritual prayers, he offers comfort and relief to those who seek his help. His work is guided by faith, intuition, and a strong connection to both nature and the spiritual world.

Alyas Toto

Alyas Toto is a folk healer known for his quiet strength and deep faith. He uses heartfelt prayers as his main tool for healing, believing that true relief comes from a higher power. With each healing session, he calls on spiritual guidance to bring peace, comfort, and recovery to those who come to him. His calm presence and sincere devotion make people feel cared for—not just physically, but spiritually as well.

Love Potion

Siquijor healers use love potions as part of their traditional spiritual practices. These potions are believed to attract affection, strengthen relationships, or rekindle lost love. They are often made from a mix of herbs, oils, roots, and sometimes personal items. The potions are usually accompanied by rituals, prayers, or chants to enhance their effectiveness, reflecting the island’s blend of folk healing and mysticism.

Agimat

Siquijor healers use agimat as protective charms or talismans believed to ward off evil spirits, bring good luck, and provide spiritual protection. These are often made from natural materials like stones, roots, metals, or inscribed objects, and are usually blessed through rituals and prayers. Agimat is worn or kept by individuals to gain strength, confidence, and protection from harm or misfortune.

Himag Oil

Siquijor healers use himag as a type of spiritual oil or potion believed to enhance one’s mystical abilities, protection, or personal power. It is often used in rituals for healing, attraction, or defense against negative energies. Made from a blend of herbs, roots, oils, and secret ingredients, himag is typically applied to the skin, objects, or used in ceremonies, and is considered a sacred part of traditional healing and spiritual practices on the island.

Massage Oil

Siquijor healers use massage oil as part of their traditional healing methods to relieve body pain, improve blood circulation, and promote relaxation. The oil is usually infused with medicinal herbs like sambong, ginger, or lagundi to enhance its therapeutic effects. During healing sessions, the oil is gently rubbed onto the body while applying traditional massage techniques, often combined with prayers or chants to boost physical and spiritual healing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is traditional healing in Siquijor?

Traditional healing in Siquijor involves the use of herbs, rituals, and faith healing techniques passed down through generations.

Are the herbal products safe to use?

Yes, the herbal products are prepared by trusted local healers and follow traditional preparation methods.

Can visitors consult the healers directly?

Yes, visitors can schedule consultations with the healers through local tourism offices or community centers.

How do Siquijor healers diagnose illnesses?

Healers use a combination of spiritual insight, pulse reading, and observation to diagnose ailments.

What are the most commonly used herbs in Siquijor healing?

Common herbs include lagundi, sambong, tawa-tawa, and bayabas, known for their healing and medicinal properties.

Are Siquijor's healing practices recognized by modern medicine?

While traditional healing is respected culturally, it is considered complementary and not a substitute for modern medical treatment.

How are herbal products prepared in Siquijor?

Herbs are often dried, boiled, or crushed to make teas, poultices, or salves following traditional recipes.

Can herbal remedies be used alongside prescription medicine?

It is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before combining herbal remedies with prescription drugs.

Are there any risks or side effects from using folk healing methods?

While many practices are safe, improper use or delays in seeking medical care can pose risks. Always use discretion and seek medical advice when necessary.
